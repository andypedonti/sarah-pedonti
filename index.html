Biography

Sarah is a Ph.D. Candidate of Applied Developmental Science and Special Education at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. She works under the mentorship of Dr. Kathryn Leech and Dr. Harriet Able investigating the language and literacy development of young children, including dual language learners and children with disabilities, in the home and school contexts. Her dissertation, funded by the ACF OPRE Head Start Research Scholars program, uses secondary data from the Migrant and Seasonal Head Start Study (Caswell et al., 2017), combined with administrative Head Start data to explore relations between disability coordination in Head Start and dual language learner's language and literacy outcomes. Sarah has worked on several IES- and OSEP-funded interinstitutional projects, including a multi-year randomized control trial of the Targeted Reading Intervention, the STEM Innovation for Inclusion in Early Education (STEMI2E2) Center, and evaluation of a project piloting smart speaker technology for parent-child dialogic reading in collaboration with the Reach Every Reader teams at MIT and the Harvard Graduate School of Education.
